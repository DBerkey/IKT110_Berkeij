How to Use

    Usage Instructions:
  1. Put the headpiece (labeled "Headpiece") on your head.
  2. Put your left and right hands on the corresponding spots on the device.
  3. Hold the device in front of you so that the white strings are under light tension.
  4. Look through the biggest hole (A) and find the object you want to measure the distance to.
    • Disclaimer: The Far-O-Nator™ is calibrated for objects of 2m height others can be used with caution. See here a list of common objects and their sizes
  5. When found, find the smallest hole fitting the object. Note this down together with if the object fitted fully, three-quarters, half, or a quarter.

    6. Example of how to hold the Far-O-Nator™


    Calculate measurement with software:
  6. Select the hole you used for measurement.
  7. Choose how much the object covered the hole (Full, Three-Quarter, Half, Quarter).
  8. Click Calculate to get the estimated distance.
  9. For advanced options, click Advanced Options to:
    • When another object size is used than 2m, set the object size (in meters).
    • When the hole-to-eye distance is different from the default 40cm, set the distance from the hole to your eye (in centimeters).
    • Enable Intergalactic Measurement to get the most accurate results.

Notes

  • All calculations are estimates and may vary based on real-world conditions or the observer's skill level.
  • Object size should be entered in meters, hole-to-eye distance in centimeters.
  • Coverage modifier helps adjust for partial coverage of the hole by the object.
